# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Before writing any claim, state which source family you are using: survey recall, CRVS stillbirth registration, facility stillbirth count, or perinatal audit workflow.
3. Name the stillbirth definition threshold before comparing anything.
4. Add evidence before interpretation.
5. Run the cheapest disconfirming test early: if two sources use different thresholds, denominators, or reporting populations, block the comparison.
6. If you claim a quality-of-care wedge, name the exact workflow: intrapartum monitoring, referral delay review, perinatal death audit, or maternity ward readiness check.
7. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask six questions:

1. What exact stillbirth measure is being claimed?
2. What threshold defines stillbirth in that source?
3. What denominator and reporting population are being used?
4. Can the source distinguish intrapartum from antepartum burden, or is that being inferred?
5. What observation would make the claim false?
6. Who could misuse the result to punish reporting facilities or staff?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether the artifact feels compelling. Ask whether it changes a named decision for a named user at a useful time without weakening reporting honesty.

Likely users for this pack are:

- a ministry maternal-newborn unit deciding whether to fund stillbirth audit and quality verification in specific regions
- a district obstetric quality-improvement team deciding whether a stillbirth signal warrants case review, fetal monitoring audit, or referral pathway audit
- a CRVS or health-information lead deciding whether the first bottleneck is counting, reconciliation, or care verification

Explicit non-use cases:

- not for punitive facility ranking
- not for attributing negligence from public counts alone
- not for cross-country comparison when thresholds and source families differ
- not for direct clinical protocol mandates without separate evidence review
