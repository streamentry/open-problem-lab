# Stillbirth Measurement Gaps And Intrapartum Care Quality

## Problem Statement

Stillbirth sits in a blind spot created by institutional boundaries and bad measurement habits. It is too often treated as a maternal-health footnote, a neonatal-health near miss, or a registration problem, depending on which system is speaking. That fragmentation is the problem.

The strongest under-attended wedge is narrower than "reduce stillbirths." It is this:

> Which high-burden countries or sub-national areas show evidence that intrapartum stillbirth burden is high enough to justify immediate verification of labor-and-delivery quality, rather than treating the observed burden mainly as a counting-system artifact?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: The UN IGME stillbirth assessment published in 2021 estimated roughly 1.9 million stillbirths globally in 2019, with the highest stillbirth rates in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia.
- Verified fact: The 2016 Lancet stillbirth series argued that many late-gestation stillbirths are preventable and that intrapartum stillbirths are a particularly policy-relevant signal because they often track failures in timely obstetric and newborn care rather than only background population risk.
- Verified fact: Household surveys, CRVS systems, and facility registers do not measure the same thing. Survey pregnancy histories capture recalled pregnancy outcomes over past periods, CRVS measures registered fetal deaths under legal definitions that vary by country, and facility records reflect service-contact populations with strong referral and denominator bias.
- Verified fact: Stillbirth receives far less routine public visibility than maternal mortality and neonatal mortality despite overlapping causal pathways and service platforms.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether public cross-country sources are sufficient to distinguish antepartum from intrapartum stillbirth at useful sub-national grain in most high-burden countries.
- Whether facility stillbirth rates can be interpreted as quality signals without linked referral, case-mix, and denominator context.
- Whether recent household survey modules measure pregnancy outcomes consistently enough across countries to support comparative ranking.
- Whether a given burden pattern should change spending first toward labor-and-delivery quality audits, fetal monitoring, referral transport, or better registration and audit systems.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Source inventory for stillbirth measurement across surveys, CRVS, and facility systems.
- Measure-family reconciliation notes that block invalid comparisons.
- Country-selection memos identifying where linked stillbirth and maternity-care data are credible enough for follow-on work.
- Claims about data availability, comparability, and decision thresholds for verification.
- Red-team review of misuse risks, especially punitive interpretation of facility stillbirth rates.
- Field-reality review with maternal-newborn quality-improvement, CRVS, or perinatal death review workflows.

This pack does not accept direct clinical protocol recommendations, facility league tables, or causal claims that a measured stillbirth gap proves negligence.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated artifact that separates at least two high-burden contexts into:

1. likely priority for labor-and-delivery quality verification
2. likely priority for counting-system strengthening
3. not yet distinguishable with public evidence

That artifact must name the decision-maker, data limits, and the specific observation that would overturn the classification.
