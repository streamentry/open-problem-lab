## Evidence Notes

### 2021 Lancet systematic assessment on stillbirth trends

Use this source for global and regional burden framing. It is the right anchor for scale and geographic concentration. It is not enough for sub-national prioritization or for distinguishing quality failure from counting failure.

### 2016 Lancet paper on ending preventable stillbirths by 2030

Use this source for the policy wedge around preventability and the special relevance of intrapartum stillbirth. Do not use it as proof that any current country has the same intervention readiness or causal mix today.

### 2016 Lancet paper on stillbirth rates and risk factors

Use this source for the argument that burden concentration and acceleration differ by region and health-system capacity. It supports pack selection. It does not solve present-day measurement comparability.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Stillbirth evidence is unusually vulnerable to definition drift, silence in administrative systems, and false comparability across source families. An evidence record is only useful here if it helps a reviewer say what the source cannot prove.
