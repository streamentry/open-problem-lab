# Antimicrobial Resistance Surveillance Gaps In LMICs

## Problem Statement

AMR killed 1.27 million people directly in 2019, with the highest burden in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. WHO GLASS collects surveillance from 127 countries but sub-national coverage is sparse — laboratories are concentrated in capitals. Linking GLASS data to lab-capacity maps reveals where the AMR burden is highest but invisible to surveillance.

> Which sub-national areas in LMICs have the highest evidence-backed AMR surveillance gaps relative to estimated AMR burden?

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: The Lancet AMR study estimated 1.27 million deaths directly attributable to AMR and 4.95 million associated deaths in 2019, with the highest burden in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia.
- Verified fact: WHO GLASS collects AMR surveillance data from 127 countries but fewer than 40 report sub-national data and fewer than 20 meet quality standards for bloodstream infection surveillance.
- Verified fact: Laboratory capacity for bacterial culture and susceptibility testing is concentrated in capital cities — most district hospitals in LMICs lack any AMR diagnostic capacity.
- Verified fact: Antibiotic consumption data is sparse in LMICs, with fewer than 30 countries reporting to WHO's antimicrobial consumption monitoring system.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether GLASS enrollment predicts actual AMR surveillance capacity on the ground.
- Whether lab-capacity maps can proxy for surveillance gaps where direct resistance data is absent.
- Whether modeled burden estimates (Murray et al., IHME GBD) are precise enough at country level to drive lab-investment prioritization.
- Whether surveillance-gap maps change laboratory-investment decisions or are used to justify antibiotic-access restrictions instead.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts work on:

- Evidence inventory and source quality assessment.
- Dataset availability including GLASS data, national AMR reports, lab-capacity surveys, and antibiotic consumption data.
- GLASS data compilation with sub-national breakdown where available.
- Laboratory-capacity mapping at facility or district level.
- Reproducible surveillance-gap analysis comparing burden to coverage.
- Red-team review of misuse including stigmatization, antibiotic-access restriction, and misattribution of modeled estimates as measured data.
- Field-reality review with laboratory-network planning context.

This pack does not accept antibiotic-access restriction recommendations, national AMR policy directives, or unvalidated gap scores presented as definitive surveillance measurements.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it can produce one reviewed and replicated sub-national AMR surveillance-gap analysis for at least two LMICs, with explicit validation limitations, that a domain reviewer judges suitable for further discussion rather than immediate policy action.
