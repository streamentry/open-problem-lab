# Evidence Notes

## Evidence Records

The machine-readable evidence list is `evidence.json`.

## Current Evidence Boundary

The initial evidence supports the existence and importance of a complete PEP access problem, not an operational ranking.

- `who-rabies-fact-sheet-2024` anchors global burden, dog-mediated transmission share, PEP components, and the distinction between vaccine access and full PEP.
- `who-gavi-rabies-vaccine-support-2024` anchors the current allocation moment: Gavi support can fund human rabies vaccine procurement and associated supplies, but not RIG or dog vaccines.
- `who-rabies-position-paper-2018` anchors WHO guidance on rabies vaccination strategy and RIG in the PEP context.
- `sreenivasan-pep-access-2019` shows that public-sector vaccine access, cost, intradermal use, distribution systems, and reporting cadence vary sharply across selected countries.
- `who-rabies-modelling-consortium-2019` supports tractability framing for PEP investment, but it is not a substitute for current country or facility evidence.
- `who-rabies-gho-topic-2026` anchors the reported-deaths indicator and WHO's warning that reported rabies data are limited by under-reporting, misdiagnosis, and under-diagnosis.
- `who-afro-rabies-prevention-control-2026` anchors current Africa-region programme framing around surveillance, PEP decision trees, case investigation, and integrated bite case management.
- `who-rabies-vaccination-immunization-page-2026` anchors exposure-category triage, RIG indication for category III exposures, and intradermal vaccination as a dose- and cost-sparing route.
- `woah-wahis-rabies-animal-health-data-2026` anchors WOAH WAHIS as the animal-rabies and dog-vaccination context source family, not a human PEP access source.
- `cdc-rabies-status-by-country-2026` is included as a rejected-for-allocation comparator: useful for understanding an external risk classification, not sufficient for endemic-country component-gap ranking.

## Evidence Gaps

- Current country applications, approvals, and delivery outcomes under Gavi rabies vaccine support.
- Public measures of RIG or monoclonal antibody availability by facility tier.
- Bite-risk triage quality and overuse versus underuse of PEP.
- PEP completion and delay between exposure and first dose.
- Linkage between human PEP demand, dog vaccination, animal bite reporting, and confirmed animal rabies.

## Suggested Next Issue

Review `source-inventory` before any allocation or ranking task. A reviewer should verify that the inventory preserves the component boundaries between vaccine procurement, RIG or monoclonal access, exposure classification, reporting cadence, patient burden, and dog-vaccination context.
