# Outputs

## Accepted Outputs

None yet.

This pack is scoped, not solved. The current evidence supports the problem and candidate care pathways, not the effectiveness of a service or technology.

## Candidate Outputs

- Source inventory separating living arrangement, family distance, functional need, care receipt, consent, and responder availability.
- Reproducible care-response-gap denominator.
- Field-verified local responder and escalation map.
- Manual concierge pilot protocol.
- Operational event ledger with unresolved events preserved.
- Unit-cost and workforce feasibility analysis.
- Red-team review covering surveillance, abuse, neglect, fraud, alert fatigue, and service withdrawal.
- Field-reality decision memo: stop, redesign, continue manually, or authorize software design.

## Publication Rule

Every output must state what it is not:

- Living alone is not proof of unmet need.
- Distant family is not proof of neglect.
- Family contact is not proof of care availability.
- Monitoring is not response.
- Acknowledgement is not resolution.
- A pilot is not clinical efficacy evidence.
- A community club is not an emergency service.
- A family willingness-to-pay statement is not proof of viable unit economics.
