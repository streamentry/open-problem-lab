# Long-Distance Elder Care And Verified Local Response In Viet Nam

## Problem Statement

Many elder-care proposals begin with a sensor, video call, or family dashboard. That starts at the wrong layer. A device can report a concern, but it cannot verify that an older person received food, took the correct medicine, reached a clinic, or received help after a fall.

The sharper problem is:

> Can Viet Nam identify older people whose family cannot respond quickly, connect them to a consented local care network, and verify that routine needs and escalations are actually resolved?

This pack separates four states that are often collapsed:

- a distant family member received information
- a local responder acknowledged a request
- a responder reached the older person
- the need was resolved or transferred to an appropriate service

Only the last two states constitute evidence of local response. None constitutes proof of clinical benefit without a reviewed study.

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: UNFPA reported in February 2026 that Viet Nam has limited access to integrated long-term care and that family care remains the dominant arrangement, with women carrying most reported care responsibility.
- Verified fact: UNFPA's 2022 care-ecosystem account reported that migration, low fertility, and modernization were weakening traditional family support, while a six-month integrated-care pilot screened about 1,500 older people and trained more than 1,000 staff and home caregivers across five provinces and Da Nang.
- Verified fact: WHO's Integrated Care for Older People guidance requires person-centred, coordinated community care and covers mobility, nutrition, sensory capacity, cognition, depressive symptoms, falls, and caregiver support.
- Verified fact: Viet Nam's Decision 1648/QD-TTg, issued on 1 August 2025, approved expansion of intergenerational self-help clubs through 2035.
- Verified fact: A 2024 World Bank field report documented 186 supported intergenerational self-help clubs across six provinces and described home visits, medication support, health monitoring, and social assistance.

## Uncertain Areas

- The current number of older people living alone or only with another older adult whose family cannot arrive within a useful response window.
- Whether census, household-survey, health, social-protection, or club records can measure family distance, functional need, and responder availability at compatible grain.
- Whether older people want a family-facing coordination service and can refuse it without pressure.
- Whether a trained local responder network can close routine and urgent requests reliably.
- Whether families will pay enough to cover travel, supervision, safeguarding, and clinical escalation.
- Whether any observed improvement comes from visits and coordination rather than monitoring technology.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts:

- dated source and dataset inventories
- reconciliation of living arrangement, functional need, family distance, and local response availability
- service-workflow mapping across families, community groups, social workers, primary care, and emergency services
- consent, privacy, safeguarding, and escalation requirements
- a reviewed manual-service pilot protocol
- red-team and field-reality reviews

This pack does not accept:

- medical diagnosis or medication changes by unlicensed workers
- claims that a wearable or dashboard prevents emergencies
- covert monitoring or family consent substituted for the older person's consent
- household risk rankings used to deny care, insurance, or benefits
- a promise of continuous emergency coverage without a staffed, audited response service

## Done Condition For V0

V0 is complete when one geographically bounded service protocol has:

1. a reproducible care-gap denominator
2. a verified responder and escalation map
3. explicit consent and safeguarding rules
4. prespecified operational metrics and stop conditions
5. independent domain, red-team, field-reality, and replication review

V0 is a field-testable protocol, not proof that the service improves health outcomes.
