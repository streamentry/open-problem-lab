## Evidence Notes

### UNFPA Viet Nam 2026

Use for current national framing on family-care dependence, gendered care responsibility, limited integrated long-term care, and policy direction. The page does not expose the underlying estimation tables, so its percentages must not become household targeting thresholds.

### UNFPA Viet Nam 2022

Use for documented pilot scope, care-planning products, training counts, and the explicit statement that more evidence is needed. Do not treat the pilot report as an impact evaluation.

### WHO ICOPE

Use as the care-domain and person-centred workflow framework. It supports coordinated assessment and caregiver support; it does not validate remote monitoring or any Vietnamese service operator.

### World Bank And JICA 2021

Use for dated demographic and long-term-care policy framing. Do not use its projections as current counts or its policy recommendation as proof of a delivery model.

### World Bank ISHC Field Report 2024

Use to establish that a community-based home-care mechanism has operated in six provinces and to name candidate service functions. Treat program totals and participant stories as limited field evidence without a counterfactual.

### Decision 1648/QD-TTg

Use to establish national policy authorization for expanding intergenerational self-help clubs through 2035. Verify local implementation separately.

## Strongest Contrary Evidence

No reviewed source proves that a remote family dashboard, wearable, or scheduled call improves health outcomes or emergency survival in Viet Nam. No reviewed source provides a current denominator combining:

- older-person living arrangement
- family travel time
- functional need
- consent preference
- local responder availability

That absence blocks market-size, safety, and impact claims.
