## Source Classification Rationale

### Usable

- **WHO Snakebite Strategy (2019)**: Official WHO publication providing the foundational burden estimates and 2030 targets. The most authoritative source for global snakebite burden framing. Limitation: modeled estimates, not direct surveillance.
- **WHO Snakebite Fact Sheet (2023)**: Updated WHO summary with revised burden ranges (81,410-137,880 deaths) and under-reporting evidence including the India community-study comparison. Limitation: global summary, no sub-national data.
- **Gutiérrez et al. 2017 (Nature Reviews Disease Primers)**: The most comprehensive peer-reviewed scientific overview of snakebite envenoming. Covers pathophysiology, epidemiology, diagnosis, treatment, and antivenom production. Limitation: published in 2017; production landscape may have shifted.

### Limited

- **Gutiérrez et al. 2014 (WHO Bulletin)**: Documents the antivenom supply crisis and proposes a multicomponent strategy. Useful for understanding structural barriers but published over a decade ago. Limitation: the supply situation has evolved since publication.
- **Lao PDR antivenom cost-effectiveness (2026)**: Provides a methodology for evaluating antivenom access interventions at sub-national level with urban-rural distributional analysis. Limitation: single-country study; methodology may not transfer directly to other contexts.
- **Africa antivenom policy analysis (2026)**: Identifies policy barriers and proposes sustainability pathways for African antivenom access. Limitation: policy analysis rather than primary data; Africa-focused, may not generalize to South Asia.

### Rejected

No sources were rejected outright. All candidate sources provide useful information within their stated limitations. No public dataset was found that provides facility-level antivenom stocking data at scale for LMICs — this is the core data gap the pack aims to surface.

## Required Dataset Properties

For any dataset accepted into canonical antivenom access-gap analysis:

- Incidence estimation methodology (facility-based, community survey, verbal autopsy).
- Geographic grain (national, sub-national, facility).
- Antivenom stocking definition (reported stock vs functional availability vs treatment outcome).
- Facility location and catchment area.
- Population denominator and agricultural-population proxy source.
- Snake species distribution and habitat model source.
- Date range and reporting lag.
- License or reuse permission.

## Rejection Rule

A dataset is rejected for canonical antivenom access-gap analysis if grain, date range, license, or incidence estimation methodology cannot be verified. Sources that provide only global estimates without sub-national granularity may be used as context but not as proof of district-level access gaps.
