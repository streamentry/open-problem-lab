# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task and one role.
3. Establish source grain and privacy boundary before matching records.
4. Add evidence before adding a quantitative claim.
5. Report missingness, unmatched records, and alternative capture assumptions.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask:

1. What is the row: crash, person, encounter, dispatch, or death?
2. What source can prove the stated claim?
3. Could one person or event appear multiple times?
4. Which road users or care-seeking groups are systematically absent?
5. Would the result change a named decision, and what could a decision-maker misuse?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether a map is visually persuasive. Ask whether a named transport or health decision-maker can use it at a defined time without confusing measurement completeness with intervention effectiveness or turning a biased record into punitive enforcement.
