# Evidence Ledger

## Current Evidence Records

The machine-readable ledger is `evidence.json`.

## Evidence Notes

### WHO Children and Digital Dumpsites

Use WHO's estimate for global risk-population framing, not as a count of confirmed exposed children or women. The report supports the need for better exposure and health data but does not provide a city-level allocation rule.

### Global E-waste Monitor 2024

Use the Monitor for material-flow scale and documented formal collection. The gap between total generation and documented formal processing is not, by itself, proof of informal processing or unsafe exposure at any particular site.

### WHO E-waste Fact Sheet

Use for a high-level synthesis of vulnerable groups and plausible health pathways. Do not infer individual causation or local thresholds from the fact sheet.

### Agbogbloshie Study

Use as site-specific primary evidence that environmental sampling and exposure assumptions matter. Do not generalize its measured concentrations or modeled risks globally.

### ILO Employment Study

Use for the labor and value-chain boundary: safer formalization must preserve the possibility of livelihoods and must be evaluated for displacement and enforcement harms.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because a source names e-waste as dangerous. It is accepted only when the source, method, geographic grain, measurement boundary, and limitations are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack.
