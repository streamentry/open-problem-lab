# Informal E-Waste Processing Exposure And Safer Formalization In Low- And Middle-Income Countries

## Problem Statement

The world's e-waste accounting is improving faster than its exposure accounting. Formal collection totals say how much material is documented, but they do not locate small dismantling, open-burning, acid-leaching, or informal resale clusters or show which pathways reach workers, children, pregnant people, food, soil, air, and water.

The sharper v0 problem is:

> Which observable signals can identify an informal e-waste processing cluster that merits primary exposure sampling or safer formalization review, without presenting a proxy map as a blood, air, soil, or health measurement?

## Discovery Case

This is a discovery candidate rather than an extension of the existing lead-exposure pack. The existing pack asks where urban lead sources may create child blood-lead risk. This pack asks how the e-waste processing pathway can be found, characterized, and connected to an intervention choice. That distinction matters because formalizing a site may reduce burning while displacing workers and contamination elsewhere; closing a site may destroy livelihoods without reducing exposure.

The strongest allocation wedge is not a global e-waste burden ranking. It is a verified cluster-level evidence protocol for a city environment director, health ministry, or funder deciding between primary sampling, worker protection, safer processing equipment, source-control enforcement, and remediation. A positive signal would justify measurement and review, not immediate relocation or health action.

## Known Facts

- Verified fact: The Global E-waste Monitor 2024 reports 62 billion kg of e-waste generated in 2022, with 22.3% documented as formally collected and recycled in an environmentally sound manner.
- Verified fact: WHO reports that as many as 18 million children and adolescents and 12.9 million women may be at risk from adverse health outcomes linked to e-waste recycling.
- Verified fact: WHO identifies children and pregnant women as particularly vulnerable to hazardous substances released by unsound e-waste recycling, while describing evidence across respiratory, neurological, developmental, thyroid, and pregnancy-related outcomes.
- Verified fact: Peer-reviewed studies at Agbogbloshie, Ghana document metal exposure and health-risk assessment around an informal e-waste processing site, but a single site study cannot establish a global exposure threshold or intervention effect.

## Uncertain Areas

- Whether open geospatial and administrative data can identify active informal processing clusters rather than ordinary dumps, repair shops, or formal recyclers.
- Which processing activities and pathways dominate exposure at a given site; proximity is not a sufficient exposure measure.
- Whether safer formalization reduces measured exposure without displacement, rebound, or livelihood harm.
- Whether municipal and national records capture transboundary flows, informal employment, seasonal activity, and children who work or live near processing sites.

## Initial Scope

This pack accepts:

- Evidence inventory and source-quality classification.
- Data inventories for e-waste flows, customs, permits, processing activity, environmental sampling, worker surveys, and child-health surveillance.
- Reproducible candidate-cluster identification with explicit validation limits.
- Exposure-pathway reviews separating air, dust, soil, water, food, take-home, and occupational routes.
- Red-team review of stigmatization, forced displacement, unsafe enforcement, and false reassurance.
- Field-reality review with municipal environment, occupational-health, waste-worker, and community perspectives.

This pack does not accept individual diagnosis, blood-lead screening mandates, site closure orders, relocation commands, or unvalidated maps presented as exposure measurements.

## Done Condition For V0

The pack is useful when it produces one reviewed and replicated cluster-level evidence artifact that distinguishes proxy identification from measured exposure and states the exact decision, user, and non-use cases.
