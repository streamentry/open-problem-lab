# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
5. Mark every operational inference as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made?
2. What source proves it?
3. What method produced it?
4. What would make it false?
5. Who could misuse it?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether an exposure map is impressive. Ask whether a national arsenic mitigation programme director or upazila health officer would change testing resource allocation based on this output, and what would go wrong if the output was wrong. Consider that exposure maps can stigmatise affected communities or discourage safe well use if alternatives are not communicated alongside risk information.
