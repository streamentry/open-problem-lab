# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Select one scoped task.
3. Add evidence before adding claims.
4. Document exposure metric, data source, and worker population denominator for every exposure estimate.
5. Keep quantitative outputs reproducible.
6. Mark every intervention recommendation as reviewed, replicated, or blocked.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask five questions:

1. What claim is being made?
2. What source proves it?
3. What method produced it?
4. What would make it false?
5. Who could misuse it?

## Field-Reality Playbook

Do not ask whether an exposure map is impressive. Ask whether it changes a named decision for a named user at a useful time, without creating worse incentives. For occupational heat stress, the named users are national labor ministries, labor inspectorates, employer associations, and worker organizations. The misuse risk is shifting blame to workers for heat injuries, justifying exclusion of heat-vulnerable workers from employment, rationalizing employer non-compliance with heat standards, or framing the problem as too complex for regulatory action.
