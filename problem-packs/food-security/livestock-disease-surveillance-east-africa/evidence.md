# Evidence Ledger

### FAO Livestock East Africa 2023

Livestock contribute 30-50 percent of agricultural GDP in East African countries. 20 million pastoralists depend on herd health. Diseases including RVF, CBPP, and PPR cause annual losses exceeding 20 percent of herd value in some areas. Source: FAO Livestock Sector Briefs.

### Anyamba et al. 2009 (PNAS)

Satellite-derived NDVI and sea-surface temperature anomalies can predict Rift Valley fever outbreak risk in East Africa with 2-3 month lead time. DOI: 10.1073/pnas.0806490106. Basis for operational RVF early warning. Limitation: predicts RVF only, not PPR or CBPP.

### WOAH WAHIS Reporting System 2024

WOAH operates WAHIS as the primary global animal disease reporting system. Collects outbreak notifications and six-monthly reports from member countries. Reporting completeness, timeliness, and sub-national spatial precision remain variable, particularly in pastoralist regions of East Africa. DOI: 10.20506/woah.3534.

### PPR Global Eradication Programme 2014

Peste des petits ruminants is distributed across large parts of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia, with an estimated 1.7 billion sheep and goats at risk. Use this source for disease-family relevance and global at-risk framing, not for current East African vaccination coverage. DOI: 10.1007/978-3-662-45165-6_13.

### CBPP Reference 2019

Contagious bovine pleuropneumonia remains endemic in sub-Saharan Africa with occurrence in at least 25 countries and causes economic losses in affected cattle populations. Use this source for disease-family relevance and broad endemicity framing, not for current WAHIS reporting completeness. DOI: 10.1007/978-3-030-25385-1_20.

### IBLI Marsabit Pilot 2009

Index-Based Livestock Insurance piloted in Marsabit, Kenya demonstrated that satellite-derived NDVI predicts livestock mortality at index scale. Establishes satellite forage indices as viable proxies for pastoralist livestock loss. DOI: 10.2139/ssrn.1844758. Limitation: validated for drought mortality, not disease mortality.

### CHIRPS Precipitation Dataset 2014

Climate Hazards Group Infrared Precipitation with Stations provides quasi-global daily to monthly rainfall at 0.05 degree resolution from 1981 to present. Standard precipitation input for drought and vegetation monitoring in East African pastoralist regions. DOI: 10.3133/ds832. Limitation: performance degrades where station networks are sparse.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence only when source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit. Every evidence record must have a stable URL (DOI or archive snapshot preferred). Sources that provide only country-level aggregates without sub-national grain are context, not proof of pastoralist-area risk. Disease impact estimates that are modeled must state the model and its validation status.
