# Playbooks

## Analyst Playbook

1. Start with `problem.json`, `evidence.json`, and `tasks.json`.
2. Separate source presence, system function, continuity, quality, and patient delivery before calculating any percentage.
3. Preserve facility level, geography, observation window, sampling frame, and missingness.
4. Add evidence before adding a quantitative output.
5. Treat all financing implications as blocked until the required reviewers and replication are recorded.

## Reviewer Playbook

Ask:

1. What exactly is the denominator: facilities, beds, patients, or oxygen episodes?
2. Is reliability observed, logged, or self-reported, and over what window?
3. Which link failed: source, power, distribution, delivery, monitoring, quality, or maintenance?
4. Could the result be an artifact of facility level, purposive sampling, missingness, or demand season?
5. Who might use this result to buy equipment that cannot be powered, maintained, or safely delivered?

## Field-Reality Playbook

The named decision-maker must be a ministry oxygen-program lead, hospital network manager, biomedical engineering unit, or funder portfolio manager. The named decision must be a measurable choice about assessment, maintenance, power backup, distribution, or procurement. A gap map alone is not a recommendation.
