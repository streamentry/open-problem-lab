# Evidence Ledger

## Current Evidence Records

The machine-readable ledger is `evidence.json`.

## Evidence Notes

### IARC Aflatoxin Monograph

Use this source for the carcinogenicity classification and cancer risk mechanism. Do not use it to estimate sub-national exposure or contamination levels.

### WHO Aflatoxin Exposure Report (2010)

Use this source for the scale of chronic exposure in developing countries and the regional comparison showing highest per-capita exposure in sub-Saharan Africa. The estimate is from 2010 and should be treated as a baseline, not current.

### Codex Alimentarius Limits

Use this source for international maximum contamination thresholds. Note that national standards in SSA countries may differ and should be checked per country before using Codex limits as the risk classification threshold.

### CDC Kenya Outbreak Report

Use this source for acute toxicity severity and extreme contamination levels. Do not generalize outbreak conditions to chronic exposure risk in other regions.

### Aflasafe Biocontrol Review

Use this source for biocontrol efficacy range and scaling evidence. Treat field trial results as upper-bound efficacy; smallholder deployment conditions may reduce effectiveness. Note author affiliation with Aflasafe development as a potential bias.

## Evidence Quality Rule

Evidence is not accepted because it sounds plausible. It is accepted when the source, method, limitations, and confidence are explicit enough for a reviewer to attack. Contamination surveys must specify test method, sampling frame, and geographic grain before they can anchor any risk estimate.
